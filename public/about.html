What We Do

Robotics for Kids is a hands-on STEM education platform that teaches children aged 8 to 16 how to build real electronic circuits, write code, and understand the technology that powers the world around them. No prior experience needed.

We don't teach kids to drag blocks on a screen. We teach them to build real circuits with real components, write real code, and solve real problems. Every student leaves with something they built with their own hands.

Our platform combines a physical robotics kit with an AI-powered web application. Students describe what they want to build in plain English, and the system generates working Arduino code, step-by-step wiring instructions, and clear explanations they can actually understand. Then they build it, upload it, and watch it come to life.

What We Teach

Core Skills

  • Circuit Building — reading schematics, using breadboards, understanding components, and wiring real circuits by hand
  • Programming — writing Arduino C/C++ code, understanding variables, loops, conditionals, functions, and sensor input/output
  • Electronics Fundamentals — voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's law, digital vs analog signals, PWM
  • Sensor Technology — how ultrasonic, light, water, and temperature sensors work and how to read their data
  • Actuators & Output — controlling LEDs, buzzers, servo motors, and understanding how code becomes physical motion
  • Problem Solving — debugging circuits, reading serial output, iterating on designs, and thinking like an engineer

The Kit

Every student works with a physical hardware kit containing real, industry-standard components:

  • Arduino Uno Microcontroller
  • Solderless Breadboard
  • Ultrasonic Distance Sensor
  • LDR (Light Sensor)
  • Water Level Sensor
  • Servo Motor (180°)
  • LEDs (Red, Green, Yellow)
  • Piezo Buzzer
  • Resistors (220Ω, 470Ω, 10kΩ)
  • Jumper Wires (M-M, M-F)
  • USB Cable
  • 9V Battery + Connector

The Platform

Our web-based lab gives students everything they need in one place:

  • AI Code Generator — describe what you want to build, get working Arduino code with wiring instructions
  • Direct Upload — compile and upload code to Arduino straight from the browser, no IDE needed
  • Serial Monitor — see real-time sensor data from the Arduino in the browser
  • AI Chat Tutor — ask questions about electronics, components, or code and get kid-friendly explanations
  • Radar Visualization — live sonar display for the radar project with distance, speed, and motor controls
  • Offline Desktop App — works without internet using local AI, perfect for classrooms with limited connectivity

Why It Works

  1. Real hardware, real results. Students hold the circuit they built. They see the LED turn on. They hear the buzzer. Learning is physical and immediate.
  2. AI removes the blank-page problem. Instead of staring at an empty IDE, students describe their idea and get working code. They learn by reading, modifying, and experimenting — not by memorizing syntax.
  3. No setup friction. No installing Arduino IDE, no driver issues, no library conflicts. Open the browser, connect the Arduino, and start building.
  4. Scales from 1 student to 100. The same platform works for a parent at home, a tutor with 5 students, or a school running 30 kits at once.
